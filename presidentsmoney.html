The Money of Ukraine's Former President


About the project

I analyzed documents found in Mezhyhirya, the residence of Ukraine's ex-president Victor Yanukovych, available on YanukovychLeaks.

These documents were dumped into a lake when the ex-president fled the country. Journalists and activists dried and scanned them. Most of the papers are accounting documents and contain proof of massive corruption, money laundering, and huge expenses which exceed Yanukovych's official income.

There are 234 567 documents. They are not OCRed, and some are of poor quality.

I took a sample of random 100 documents and manually input the data on this spreadsheet. I categorized the financial operations mentioned and counted the totals. I also flagged the most interesting documents and studied them.

Results

The 100 documents contain records of operations worth $ 25,599.409 and give a clear idea of Yanukovych's lifestyle. Some of the documents contain proof of money laundering and other unlawful operations.

God knows what can be found in the remaining 234,467 documents.



87% of the total sum I found (more than $ 22 million) weas spent on building real estate objects. They include the ex-president's residence Mezhyhirya, twice the size of Buckingham Palace, several offices in downtown Kiev, hunting clubs, parliament and party offices etc.

What is wrong with this multi-million construction?

Most of the projects were undertaken by company named Tantalit. It is owned by the brother of Yanukovych's chief of staff, although many believe ex-president himself controls the company. Not to mention that its records were found in his home.

However, ex-president couldn't afford any of his estates. According to his latest available income declaration, Yanukovych's made $ 96,859 a year.

In public, he always denied his participation in any kind of business and never reported owning luxurious property. Nor did he let any journalists inside Mezhyhirya.



Building posh estates wasn't the only obsession Ukraine's former president had. The expenditures not connected to construction total $3 million in the sample. This money gives an inside to ex-president's lifestyle, his hobbies and passions.

The documents are checks for purchases for decorating the house (like hundreds of thousands od dollars spent on marble floors), transfers to his mistress's charity fund (which is supposed to help children, but was noticed in suspicious financial operations including government funding), salaries of his employees and trusted people (ranging from $93,163 per month for his head of security to $700 per month for construction workers), financial activity of his hunting clubs and so on.




10 amazing things I found in Yanukovych's documents

1. A floor cleaner machine
Found in the documents is a floor cleaner machine worth $19,128. The model mentioned in the pay check Tennant T5 Fast is searchable online and can be bought on eBay for $8 500. Not only did the president cheat on his country. His employees cheated on him.
2. Elite hunting
Hunting was a passion of Yanukovych. He owned a hunting club called Kedr, whose memeber's were billionaires and ruling elite. Membership fees were $ 100,000, as found in the documents.
The land for former president's hunting clubhouses was granted in a shadowy way and against the will of environment activists, concerned about the fate of endangered species. Also, as recently found, millions of tax-payers money covered some needs of the wealthy hunters, like roads and animal enclosures.
3. An address plaque
How much can this street plaque cost? $798 if it has a president's address on it. Yanukovych is well-known for his love for posh things, but with this sign and the money spent he could do better.
4. Marble, doors and chairs
Ukraine' former president loved marble. Found: marble floors worth $32,400 only for one of the bathrooms. He also had marble walls and tables. In the 100 documents I analyzed the marble purchases totaled $374,195. Yanukovych also loved extravagant chairs (found one in a guest house for $ 664) and doors (there's a record of doors worth $115,370).
5. A million dollar bodyguard
Meet a unique man who makes $93,153 a month (over a million a year). He is Anatoliy Kobylinskiy, ex-president's body-guard an owner of a company providing security services. Kobylinskiy was so appreciated by Yanukovych, that he even became a member of a local council and received a state award in 2013.
According to the documents, ex-president even covered his body-guard's professional training: gave $ 4,249 in cash for Kobylinskiy to travel to a shooting competition in Hungary.
6. The mistress, the son and the Russian Orthodox church
Lubov, ex-president's lover, has a charity fund. It was supposed to help children, but many people suspect it was laundering money. I found documents proving that the fund received a $ 11,828 transfer from Yanukovych's company. Another $ 9,420 were transferred from a bank, owned by ex-president's son.

When it giving away money, Miss Polezhay was a pinchpenny. There are few mentions of her expenses: $110 for tea and candies, $ 374 for a 1000 balloons.
Oh, and the Russian Orthodox church. I found a note in which the church fund asked Lubov to make a donation of $ 1 875. She approved.
7. Sauna with a shooting gallery
Found in the documents are the $1,438.726 costs for a "sauna with a shooting gallery". I have no idea why people have to shoot in a sauna, but this isn't the only weird thing about Yanukovych and his friends.
8. Millions for the fence
A mysterious person reported cash expenses worth $ 19,7 millions. There's a date - August 2008 and 31 items mentioned in this list.
Among them is the $ 4,621.960 cost of the outer fence of Mezhyhirya, the ex-president's residence. This fence was everything I and millions of citizens saw for many years. Even the journalists were not let inside the estate.
9. Animals
Apart from hunting, Yanukovych wasn't opposed to living animals as well. He liked to have them around in his estates, closed in cages. In Mezhyhirya, the horse stables cost $ 150 091. The fence of the deer garden was even more expensive - $91,104.
In a hunting clubhouse Syholychie, just the dog and bird enclosures totalled $776,349.
10. Arms, kickboxing and a bow!
I found a document containing purchases for ex-president's security needs. Among sniper's uniforms and car rent for cynologists, there was it: a bow, worth $2,685. I am still not sure why would Yanukovych need a bow. There's hope it's for sports. Former president didn't look very fit, but he liked sports gear: found in the other documents was a leg protector for kickboxing ($131) and swimming goggles ($180).
